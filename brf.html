Bromine Monofluoride Infrared Spectrum

Suppose the infrared spectrum of 75Br19F shows an intense line at 380 cm−1. Let’s calculate the force constant k for this diatomic molecule.

Derivation

  1. For a diatomic molecule treated as a harmonic oscillator, its fundamental vibrational frequency (in s−1) is
    ν = (1 / 2π) √( k / µ ).
  2. Solving for k gives:
    k = (2π ν)2 · µ.
  3. We often measure IR frequencies in wavenumbers, ν̃ (cm−1) rather than Hz. Since ν = c ν̃,
    k = (2π c ν̃)2 · µ.

Thus, once you know the reduced mass µ and the wavenumber ν̃, you can directly compute k.

Solution Example

The reduced mass for 75Br19F (in kg) is found by:

µ = [ (75.0 amu) × (19.0 amu) / (75.0 + 19.0) amu ] × (1.661 × 10−27 kg·amu−1) = 2.52 × 10−26 kg

Using ν̃ = 380 cm−1:

k = [ 2π (2.998 × 108 m·s−1) (380 cm−1) (100 cm·m−1) ]2 × (2.52 × 10−26 kg)
= 129 kg·s−2 = 129 N·m−1.
